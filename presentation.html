#2 Ticketing and Tasking

Because you've gotta get sh*t done!

Jira is used by many large companies, Part of atlassian's productsuite (Confluence, Bitbucket). "#1 software tool used by agile teams"
  • Bug tracking, Issue Tracking, Project Management
  • Ticketing, Issues, communication throughout divisions
  • Built in ticketing timer
  • Let’s you prioritize, assign, track, report and audit your “issues"
  • Improves productivity by cutting down on time wasted on tracking issues and coordination
  • Sprint organization. Task list. All open issues moving to planning

Asana

asana.com

Many large brands both tech and not are using Asana now

  • An easy way for teams to track their work and get results
  • Aim at supporting a completely diverse work environment
  • "Rid the world of busy work"

#3 Collaboration

There's no "I" in team

“Secure, effortless collaboration from any device. Box makes it easy to manage and share files across your business."
  • Full service cloud storage service
  • Box has been around, and has focused on business from the start
  • Similar to dropbox, but made specifically for businesses, not that dropbox isn't used in companies
  • Box is dollar for dollar a better deal than Dropbox and offers more storage.
  • Dropbox has an enterprise offering, Dropbox for business that is still catching on
  • 34,000 organizations paying for this service.
“Get to done together, faster. Spend less time hunting things down and more time making things happen."
  • Wiki software used by more than half of fortune 100 companies.
  • 13,200 companies use this worldwide.
  • Create anything your team needs - meeting notes, product requirements, knowledge base articles - on the web so everyone can contribute
  • Made this list because it is in the same suite as Jira
  • Part of atlassian's productsuite (Jira, Bitbucket).

GitHub

GitHub Issues

  • And now, one we will all be using very soon
  • Kind of falls into all categories, minus communication
  • Most software projects have a bug tracker of some kind. GitHub’s tracker is called Issues, and has its own section in every repository.
  • Great way to keep track of tasks, enhancements, and bugs for your projects. They’re kind of like email—except they can be shared and discussed with the rest of your team.
  • Focus on collaboration
